Transcript

Welcome to Rad Menus Podcast. Today, we will be discussing the most important foods to have on hand at college in order to be motivated to stay healthy.

The first step is to get rid of ramen and microwaveable macaroni and cheese and substitute those for healthier, tastier foods. Contrary to popular belief, it’s not time consuming to be healthy and it certainly isn’t too expensive. There are easy ways to stay healthy throughout college that transfers to your life after school. This podcast is going to discuss healthy options in the dining halls, grocery stores, and farmers markets that won’t take too much effort or money from you. 

Mostly everyone on a college campus has the meal plan, but that doesn’t mean you have to eat burgers and fries everyday because that’s all you think is available. 

Many dining halls have a myriad of vegetables seasoned to appeal to any tastebuds. Fruit cups or salads are an easy grab for on the go. Another tip is to substitute fish or chicken for red meat.

If you don’t have a meal plan, the grocery store is the best option. These are some foods you should have on hand:

  • Trail mix with different nuts, dried fruit, and M and Ms for a sweet treat. 
  • Fruits such as bananas and apples that are easy to grab on the go. 
  • Popcorn, the college kid’s staple food group. 
  • Peanut butter
  • Yogurt in freezer - healthier alternative to ice cream
  • Frozen/fresh veggies with little to no sauce covering them.
  • String cheese
  • Jerky 
  • Dark chocolate

The farmers market isn’t just guaranteed fresh, it is fresh out of the garden. All fresh fruits and vegetables can be found there, along with freshly cut meats and flowers.

Make meals on Sunday for the week and pack them in small containers to take to snack on between classes. Overall, these tips will help you make healthier decisions throughout the week.
